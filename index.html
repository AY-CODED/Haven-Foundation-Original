Empowering the Future, One Life at a Time

The Young Women & Child Haven Foundation is a nonprofit organization dedicated to protecting, uplifting, and empowering vulnerable young women and children. Our foundation was born from a deep desire to create a safe, nurturing environment where hope is restored, dignity is preserved, and potential is unlocked.

We believe every child and young woman deserves a chance to thrive, regardless of their circumstances. Through our programs, we provide education, healthcare, mentorship, and life skills training to help them build brighter futures.

About Us

The Young Women & Child Haven Foundation was established in 2015 with the mission to protect, uplift, and empower vulnerable young women and children. Our organization provides safe spaces, education, healthcare, and life skills training to help beneficiaries build sustainable futures. We work in partnership with local communities, government agencies, and other NGOs to create lasting change.

Our Story

A few years back, God gave me a project to work on. Although I knew I was meant to undertake it, I was scared to start and doubted my capacity to handle such a significant task, even though I had already navigated similar experiences. Fear held me back, making me question whether I could truly succeed.
Over time, I realized that God would never assign me a huge project without equipping me with the necessary abilities. Last year, in 2023, I met Mr. @tunde_rhema, the CEO of @tr_leadershiphub. He gave me a scholarship for one of their programs. Despite having other business ventures that needed clarification, this project was the only one that felt right to focus on. Together, we developed a blueprint outlining how the project could work and identified the key elements to include.
Even after everything was prepared, I found many reasons not to start because fear crept in again. I began to ask myself if I was truly ready, so I left the project. About two months ago, the Holy Spirit started reminding me of the project I had neglected, and it became a burden on my heart. Just three weeks ago, during a service at Menorah, while our pastor was ministering, I heard the Holy Spirit speak to me in a clear voice, as if He were sitting right next to me. He said, "I took you through that process to help others." My heart melted, and I was broken. At that moment, I knew I couldn't run anymore. Right then and there, I began to ask for strength because I knew I couldn't do it all by myself.
We are a community whose foundation is built on God's instructions to help people through the pain and process that he took us through.
